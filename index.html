Introduction

Aloe vera (Aloe barbadensis Mill.) is one of the most important medicinal plants used worldwide. It is valued for its antioxidant, antimicrobial, wound-healing, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory properties.

Despite its medicinal value, Aloe vera is highly susceptible to fungal diseases which negatively affect yield, quality, and safety. This research focuses on isolating and identifying fungal pathogens and understanding their impact.

Medicinal and Economic Importance

Aloe vera plays a vital role in pharmaceutical, cosmetic, food, and herbal industries. It supports rural livelihoods and contributes significantly to the herbal economy.

Fungal infections reduce market value, cause rejection of raw material, and pose risks due to mycotoxin contamination.

Botanical and Phytochemical Profile

  • Acemannan: Immunomodulatory polysaccharide
  • Aloin & Aloe-emodin: Antimicrobial compounds
  • Fatty acids: Oleic, linoleic, palmitic acids
  • Vitamins: A, C, E and B-complex

Clinical Pathology of Aloe vera

  • Leaf spot disease
  • Tip dieback
  • Collar and root rot
  • Mycotoxin contamination

Disease Cycle of Fungal Pathogens

  1. Survival of spores in soil or debris
  2. Dispersal by wind and water
  3. Spore germination
  4. Penetration through wounds or stomata
  5. Colonization and symptom development
  6. Secondary sporulation

Major Fungal Pathogens Identified

  • Alternaria alternata
  • Fusarium oxysporum
  • Curvularia lunata
  • Nigrospora oryzae
  • Aspergillus niger
  • Penicillium species

Research Methodology

Samples were surface sterilized and inoculated on Potato Dextrose Agar (PDA). Plates were incubated at 25–28°C and pure cultures were obtained.

Identification was carried out using colony morphology and microscopic examination of spores and hyphae.

Scientific Analysis

Results indicated that Alternaria alternata was the most dominant pathogen. Disease severity was strongly influenced by humidity and soil moisture.

Integrated Disease Management

  • Biocontrol agents
  • Botanical extracts
  • Improved cultivation practices
  • Integrated Disease Management (IDM)

Impact of Climate Change

Climate change increases disease severity by creating favorable conditions for fungal growth. Rising temperatures and humidity are major contributors.

Industrial and Societal Applications

This research helps ensure quality control in herbal and pharmaceutical industries and supports sustainable agriculture.

Ethical and Laboratory Safety

Safe handling of fungal cultures and proper disposal of infected material is essential to avoid contamination and environmental risk.

Future Scope

Future work may include molecular identification, AI-based disease detection, and development of eco-friendly biofungicides.

Conclusion

Fungal diseases pose a serious threat to Aloe vera cultivation. Sustainable, eco-friendly disease management strategies are essential for long-term productivity and safety.
