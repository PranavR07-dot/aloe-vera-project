Introduction

Aloe vera is a globally recognized medicinal plant widely used in pharmaceutical, cosmetic, and food industries. Despite its therapeutic value, Aloe vera is highly susceptible to fungal diseases that reduce plant quality, yield, and medicinal efficacy.

About Aloe vera

Aloe vera (Aloe barbadensis Miller) is a perennial succulent plant belonging to the family Liliaceae. It contains bioactive compounds such as aloin, aloe emodin, and acemannan which exhibit antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound healing properties.

Aim & Objectives

Aim: To isolate and identify fungal pathogens responsible for diseases in Aloe vera.

  • Collection of infected plant samples
  • Isolation of fungal pathogens
  • Microscopic identification
  • Pathogenicity testing
  • Documentation of fungal diversity

Materials and Methods

Materials

  • Infected Aloe vera leaves
  • Potato Dextrose Agar (PDA)
  • Laboratory equipment
  • Sterilization chemicals

Methods

  1. Sample collection
  2. Surface sterilization
  3. Fungal isolation on PDA
  4. Pure culture preparation
  5. Microscopic identification
  6. Pathogenicity testing

Common Fungal Pathogens Identified

Alternaria alternata
Fusarium oxysporum
Fusarium solani
Aspergillus niger
Penicillium spp.
Nigrospora oryzae

How Fungal Pathogens Infect Aloe vera

  1. Fungal spores land on leaf surface
  2. Germination under humid conditions
  3. Hyphal penetration of plant tissue
  4. Enzyme and toxin secretion
  5. Leaf spots, rot, and tissue damage
